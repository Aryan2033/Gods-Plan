========================vectorization mindset==============================================

in Ai/ml data is often
-in millions or row
-high gigabyte
-processed repeadetly

using normal python loops becomes very slow

so engineers use:
  - vectorized operations
  - column operations
  - optimized c backed libraries like pandas and numpy


1] map()  :
        In pandas, Series.map() works similarly on one column.

2] apply():
         apply() is more flexible. It can run a function on a pandas Series or DataFrame, and you can choose whether it works row-wise or column-wise

         Why use them instead of loops:

They are shorter and easier to read.
They express the intent clearly: “apply this function to every item.”
In pandas, they can be faster or better optimized than a manual Python loop.

however they are not always faster 
or pure Python, map() is often just a cleaner style choice. In pandas, true vectorized operations like df["a"] * 2 are usually faster than apply().
