The 450-Day "High-Pay" Roadmap (2026–2027)
​Phase 1: Software Craftsmanship (Months 1–4)
• ​Goal: Move from "theory" to "production." Write code that a professional German team can read, maintain, and scale.
• ​Key Skills: Python OOP (Classes/Inheritance), Type Hinting, GitFlow (Branching), and FastAPI for model serving.
• ​Target: Build one library-style ML project on GitHub that follows professional standards.

​Phase 2: MLOps & The Werkstudent Bridge (Months 5–8)
• ​Goal: Become an engineer who knows how to deploy. In 2026, the highest salaries go to those who bridge ML and Infrastructure.
• ​Key Skills: Docker, CI/CD (GitHub Actions), and Cloud (AWS S3/EC2).
• ​The Move: Apply for Werkstudent roles at ZEISS (Oberkochen), Bosch, or Mercedes-Benz.
• ​Salary Target: €15–€22/hour as a student; build the reputation for the full-time role.

​Phase 3: Specialization—Agentic AI & Industrial AI (Months 9–12)
• ​Goal: Master the 2026 market trends. 40% of enterprise apps now use AI Agents.
• ​Key Skills: LangGraph/CrewAI for agents, RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) with Vector DBs (Qdrant), and PyTorch for computer vision (huge in the Stuttgart region).
• ​Trend: Master the EU AI Act compliance basics—this is a "must-have" for German corporations now.

​Phase 4: The Closer—Industrial Thesis & Career (Months 13–15)
• ​Goal: Convert your degree into a €95,000+ starting salary.
• ​The Move: Secure an Industrial Master’s Thesis at a Top-Tier company.
• ​Final Skills: ML System Design (scaling to millions of users) and Salary Negotiation.
